Explanation for slide 61

The above story is actually typical of all real world control system problems. One needs: better sensors (to see the state of the system); better actuators (to change the state of the system) and better control algorithms (to provide the required finesse when connecting sensors to actuators). The last aspect is crucial. Indeed, we are certain that the reader will know many people who have good eyes (sensors) and strong muscles (actuators) but who cannot play tennis at the highest international level because they lack the required hand-eye coordination (i.e. they do not have sufficiently sophisticated control algorithms).
