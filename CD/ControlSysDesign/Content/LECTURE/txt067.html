We will thus turn, finally, to the issue of control design subject to constraints. Actually, the literature on this topic can be (roughly) divided into two streams. One is based on simple (ad-hoc) ideas which have their origins in the anti-windup scheme for a PI controller shown on the left of this slide. Another stream uses on line methods to solve receding horizon optimal control problems. The latter idea goes under the name, Model Predictive Control.
