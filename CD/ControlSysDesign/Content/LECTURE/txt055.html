Explanation for slide 55

Well, if you manipulate the previous expression a little, then you get the fundamental constraint shown on this slide. In fact this says that the hold-up effect is entirely due to those pesky imaginary axis zeros. Moreover, (and this is the key issue), because the limitation is fundamental, no control system (within the given architectures) can overcome the problem. We thus see how important fundamental limitations are. Without the insight they provide, one could spend hours (maybe years) trying to design a controller (fuzzy, adaptive, nonlinear, … ) to overcome the problem. However, we know, a priori, that all must fail !
