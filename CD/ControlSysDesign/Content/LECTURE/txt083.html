In summary, we have discussed a number of real world design problems ranging from pH control, to continuous casting of metal, to rolling mills and finally food processing. We have seen that, as one delves more deeply into these problems, so one is inevitably forced to consider nonlinear issues (including nonlinear dynamics and constraints on actuators and states). Moreover, to carry out a design in the face of these nonlinearities, requires an appreciation of fundamental limitations.
