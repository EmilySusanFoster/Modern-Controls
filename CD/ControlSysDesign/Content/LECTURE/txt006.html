Explanation for slide 6

Results on fundamental limitations for linear feedback systems have a long history - beginning with Bode's own work. A flavour of some of the results is given here.

In particular, the first result shows that S and T cannot be simultaneously made small.

The third result says that our ability to reduce T below 1 (where log T becomes zero) is made more difficult by the presence of right half plane zeros (qi on the slide) and pure delays in the plant ( t on the slide).

The second last result shows that the maximum value of S (as a function of frequency) cannot be less than one if the system has a right half pane zero.

The last result shows that the maximum value of T cannot be less than one if the system is open loop unstable.
